🔍 1. What This Tool Does

This tool helps Atlassian employees in India calculate the values required to fill Schedule FA — Section A3 (Details of Foreign Equity and Debt Interest) in their Indian Income Tax Return (ITR).

When you receive RSUs (Restricted Stock Units) from Atlassian, you must disclose these as foreign assets in your ITR every year — even if you haven't sold any shares. Failure to disclose can attract penalties up to ₹10 lakh per year under the Black Money Act.

What the tool calculates:

  • Initial Investment (₹) — Value of shares on the vesting date (net shares × FMV × SBI TT Buy Rate)
  • Peak Value (₹) — Highest value of your holding during the calendar year
  • Closing Balance (₹) — Value of shares held on December 31
  • Gross Sale Proceeds (₹) — Proceeds from any voluntary share sales (not sell-to-cover)
📌 Note: Schedule FA follows the Calendar Year (January 1 – December 31), not the Indian Financial Year (April – March).

👤 2. Who Should Use This Tool

This tool is designed for:

  • Atlassian employees in India who are Resident and Ordinarily Resident (ROR)
  • Employees who hold or have held Atlassian RSUs at any point during the calendar year
  • Employees filing ITR-2 or ITR-3 (required when you have foreign assets)
⚠️ Note: If you are a Non-Resident (NR) or Resident but Not Ordinarily Resident (RNOR), Schedule FA may not apply to you. Consult your CA.

✅ 3. Before You Start

What you need:

  1. Shareworks Account Report (Excel)

    Download the Account Report from Shareworks by following these exact steps:

    1. Log in to shareworks.solium.com
    2. Click on Activity in the top navigation menu
    3. Select Reports from the dropdown
    4. Click on Account Report
    5. Click Download or Export to save as .xlsx

    The downloaded file will contain multiple sheets. The tool uses the "Award Transactions" sheet which has your complete RSU vesting history including vesting dates, shares vested, shares held, FMV per share, and sell-to-cover details.

    💡 Tip: Make sure you download the full report (not just a partial view). The file should have sheets named: Award Information, Award Transactions, Sales, Share Holdings, Employee ID & Name, Quote History.

  2. Know your Assessment Year

    For the current filing season (July 2026), you are filing for AY 2026-27, which covers Calendar Year 2025 (Jan 1 – Dec 31 2025).

  3. A modern web browser

    Chrome, Firefox, Safari, or Edge. No installation required — the tool runs entirely in your browser.

🚀 4. Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Download your Shareworks Account Report

    Log in to Shareworks → Click Activity → Select Reports → Click Account Report → Download as Excel (.xlsx). Make sure the report includes all historical vestings (from inception).

  2. Open the ITR FA A3 Tool

    Visit the tool homepage. No login required.

  3. Select your Assessment Year

    Use the Assessment Year dropdown to select the year you are filing for. For current filing (July 2026), select AY 2026-27 (CY 2025).

  4. Upload your Shareworks Excel file

    Click "Choose File" and select your downloaded Account Report Excel file. The tool will automatically parse the Award Transactions sheet.

  5. Click "Calculate"

    The tool will fetch live SBI TT Buy rates and TEAM stock prices, then compute all required FA A3 values. This takes 5–10 seconds.

  6. Review the results

    Check the on-screen results table. Each row represents one RSU vesting lot. Verify the dates and quantities match your Shareworks statement.

  7. Download the Excel output

    Click "Download Excel" to get a formatted Excel file with 4 sheets: ITR Entry, Table A2 (Custodian), Calculation Audit, and Compliance Notes.

  8. Have your CA review the output

    Share the downloaded Excel with your Chartered Accountant before entering values in the ITR portal.

  9. Enter values in ITR portal

    Log in to incometax.gov.in → File ITR-2/ITR-3 → Go to Schedule FA → Section A3 → Enter each row from the "ITR_Entry_A3" sheet.

🔄 7. Sell-to-Cover (STC) Treatment

When RSUs vest, Atlassian automatically sells some shares to cover your Indian tax liability (TDS). This is called Sell-to-Cover (STC).

✅ CA-Confirmed Approach (used by this tool):
Only the net shares received (after STC) are reported in Schedule FA A3.
STC shares are ignored entirely — they are not counted as a sale, and do not appear in "Gross sale proceeds."

Example: 87 shares vested → 32 sold as STC → 55 net shares reported.

The STC shares are accounted for in your Form 16 as part of TDS deducted on the perquisite value. There is no double taxation.

🖥️ 9. Entering Values in the ITR Portal

  1. Log in to incometax.gov.in

    Go to e-File → Income Tax Returns → File Income Tax Return. Select ITR-2 (salaried with foreign assets).

  2. Navigate to Schedule FA

    In the ITR form, scroll to Schedule FA → Table A3.

  3. Add one row per vesting lot

    Click "Add Row" for each entry in the ITR_Entry_A3 sheet. Enter values exactly as shown — no commas in text fields, dates in YYYY-MM-DD format.

  4. Fill Table A2 (Custodian account)

    Use values from the Table_A2_Custodian sheet. Enter your Shareworks account number and opening date manually.

  5. Complete Schedule FSI and CG

    If you had voluntary share sales, report capital gains under Schedule CG. Report the same income under Schedule FSI with country code 840 (USA). File Form 67 if you had any foreign tax withheld.

⚠️ Portal Tips:
  • Do not include commas in text fields (Address, Name)
  • Enter amounts in whole rupees (round off decimals)
  • Country code for USA is 2
  • Date format must be YYYY-MM-DD
  • Nature of entity: Software Services

❓ 10. Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What FX rate is used for conversions?
The tool uses the SBI Telegraphic Transfer Buying Rate (TT Buy Rate) as mandated by Rule 115 of the Income Tax Rules, 1962. This is the rate at which SBI buys USD from you (when your USD income flows into India). TT Buy is different from TT Sell rate — using TT Sell would be incorrect.
Q: I have shares vested in 2022 that I still hold. Do I include them?
Yes. Schedule FA requires disclosure of all shares held at any point during the calendar year. The tool automatically includes prior-year lots that are still in your holding. Each vesting lot appears as a separate row.
Q: What if my closing balance is zero because all shares were sold?
Still report the lot in Schedule FA. Enter the initial value, peak value, closing balance = 0, and the full sale proceeds in "Total gross proceeds from sale." A sold position is not an exemption from disclosure.
Q: Should I use ITR-1, ITR-2, or ITR-3?
ITR-1 and ITR-4 do NOT have Schedule FA. You must use ITR-2 (if salaried with no business income) or ITR-3 (if you have business income).
Q: What is the "peak value"?
The peak value is the highest value of your holding on any single day during the calendar year. The tool finds the day when TEAM's stock price was highest during the year, and multiplies it by your net shares on that day × the SBI TT Buy rate for that date.
Q: My data doesn't load — I see "using bundled fallback prices"
This happens when the tool is opened locally (from your Downloads folder) due to browser security restrictions. Use the live site at https://arpanagr.github.io/itr-schedule-fa/ for full functionality with live data.
Q: Is this tool officially approved by Atlassian or the Income Tax Department?
No. This is an unofficial educational tool built to help employees understand the calculations. It is NOT endorsed by Atlassian or the Income Tax Department. Always consult a qualified CA before filing.
Q: What about shares sold voluntarily (not sell-to-cover)?
Voluntary sales are captured from the Sales sheet in your Shareworks report. The proceeds are shown in the "Total gross proceeds from sale" column. You must also report these as Capital Gains in Schedule CG of your ITR.
